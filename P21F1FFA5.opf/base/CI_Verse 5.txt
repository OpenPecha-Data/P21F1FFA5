Moreover: Now, if [you say that] you deny the things after having apprehended them through perception,
[we reply:] that perception through which the things are apprehended does not exist.

You cannot say that you deny all things in the statement ’All things are void’, after having
apprehended them through perception.Why? Because even perception, an instrument of true cognition,
is void, being included in all things. The person who apprehends the things is alse
void.Thus, there is no such thing as apprehension through perception, an instrument of
true cognition; and a negation of that which is not apprehended is a logical impossibility.
In these circumstances, your statement that all things are void is not valid.You think,
perhaps, that you deny all things after having apprehended them through inference, verbal
